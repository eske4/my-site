Creating and Evaluating Intelligent Systems


In collaboration with a group of 5, we were assigned the task of developing an interactive intelligent system. Our project focused on a mobile application designed to generate cycling or walking routes based on points of interest. The project encompassed the entire product development process, from the initial ideation to the creation of the final prototype.

We followed the Double Diamond method, progressing through the Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver phases. During the Discover phase, we delved into interactive intelligent systems, generated ideas, and conducted thorough research. Moving into the Define phase, we refined our concept, prioritized requirements, and formulated a clear problem statement.

My responsibilities during the development phase were the backend development in Unity, architecting APIs using Python's Flask framework, and setting up a database with PostgreSQL through the cloud service Supabase. Throughout this phase, I additionally conducted usability tests, gathering essential feedback for use in iterative improvements. The adoption of a Kanban workflow for task organization proved effective, and the project, along with a dedicated course, expanded my proficiency in conducting tests

The users expressed enthusiasm for exploring new neighbourhoods with the application together with useful feedback for future enhancements. Suggestions included implementing unobtrusive audio guidance, addressing music playback interruptions, and introducing user-preference-based sorting for points of interest.

The project equipped me with a versatile skill set in frontend, backend, and databases. While Python served well for efficient feature testing, the project also highlighted its limitations for API development. Moving forward, I aim to explore alternative languages for APIs while continuing to leverage Python's strengths in swift feature testing.
